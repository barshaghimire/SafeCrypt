About E-Waste Management

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices. Proper e-waste management helps reduce environmental harm, conserves resources, and promotes sustainable practices.

Do's

  • Recycle e-waste through authorized centers.
  • Backup and erase data from devices before disposal.
  • Donate functional devices to those in need.
  • Segregate e-waste from regular garbage.
  • Educate others about e-waste recycling.

Don'ts

  • Do not dispose of e-waste in general trash bins.
  • Avoid dismantling devices without expertise.
  • Refrain from burning e-waste as it releases toxins.
  • Do not store unused devices indefinitely.
  • Never mix e-waste with biodegradable waste.

Benefits of E-Waste Recycling

Discover the positive impacts that e-waste recycling can have on the environment and society.

Environmental Protection

Recycling e-waste reduces the harmful effects of toxic chemicals on the environment.

Resource Conservation

Recycling conserves precious materials like gold, silver, and copper from old devices.

Energy Savings

Recycling e-waste uses less energy compared to extracting raw materials from the earth.

Health and Safety

Proper disposal of e-waste ensures safer communities by reducing the release of hazardous substances.
